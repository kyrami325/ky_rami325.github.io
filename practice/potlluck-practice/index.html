Rainbow Kitten Surprise

 

Albums by RKS

Boone, North Carolina-based Rainbow Kitten Surprise (RKS) is an alternative rock/indie band. College students Sam Melo and Darrick "Bozzy" Keller started the band in 2013 after starting to compose and record music in their dorm rooms at Appalachian State University. Since then, Ela Melo on vocals, Ethan Goodpaster on electric guitar, and Jess Haney on drums have joined the group, along with other musicians.

     


In addition to having three full-length albums out, including How to: Friend, Love, Freefall, RKS is well-known for their live performances. Artists such as Frank Ocean, Schoolboy Q, Kings of Leon, and Modest Mouse had a great influence on their sound. Some claim that their lyrics contain both subtle and overt references to the Bible, which may come naturally to lead singer Sam Melo as he spent a lot of his childhood with his missionary parents in the Caribbean.

DESCRIPTION

Early in 2013, the band's debut EP, Mary, was released by lead singer Sam Melo and guitarist and backup vocalist Darick "Bozzy" Keller before they came together as the present Rainbow Kitten Surprise.

Before releasing their debut album, Seven, the pair would go on to add members Ethan Goodpaster (lead guitar), Charlie Holt (bass), and Jess Haney (drums). The two projects were swiftly merged and reissued under the name Seven + Mary.

Mary, which makes up the final four tracks of Seven + Mary, stands apart from the rest of the record due to its sparse instrumentals and straightforward song structure.

DESCRIPTION

The 2018 album How To: Friend, Love, Freefall by Rainbow Kitten Surprise delves into topics of friendship, love, and loss. The record serves as a guide through the joy and warmth of love and friendship, as well as the anguish and sorrow that might accompany them. The songs on the album are full with history, melancholy, and optimism, as the title suggests. Additionally, there are more overt religious overtones on the album than there were in the group's earlier releases.

The album's 36 minutes are filled with complex melodies and profound lyrics. Instrumentalists Derick "Bozzy" Keller, Ethan Goodpaster, Charlie Holt, and Jess Haney accompany the band's lead singer, Ela Melo. Some claim that the album shows the band's development, with each song enhancing the other.

DESCRIPTION

Rainbow Kitten Surprise's fourth studio album is titled Love Hate Music Box. Through pop, electronic, rock, and hip-hop, among other genres, the album delves into the intricacies of relationships and life's ups and downs. Lead singer Ela Melo, lead producer Konrad Snyder (Noah Kahan), and producer Daniel Tashian (Kacey Musgraves) produced the album.

"Peter Pan," "Overtime," "Superstar," "Lucky," and "LOL" are among the 22 tracks on the album. Kacey Musgraves is included on the album "Overtime," which some claim keeps listeners on their toes by experimenting with various sounds. The album has received high marks from critics, and some claim the band's music is still evolving.
